Monas - Jakarta

Monas, short for "Monumen Nasional" (National Monument), is an iconic symbol of Indonesia's independence and pride. Located in the heart of Jakarta, this towering structure stands at 132 meters and is crowned by a gold-coated flame, representing the spirit of freedom.

Construction of Monas began in 1961 under President Sukarno's administration and was completed in 1975. The monument is surrounded by the Merdeka Square, a large park often used for public gatherings and events.

Visitors to Monas can explore the museum at its base, which showcases Indonesia's struggle for independence. The observation deck at the top offers breathtaking panoramic views of Jakarta's skyline.

Monas is not only a historical landmark but also a vibrant cultural attraction, hosting festivals, events, and exhibitions throughout the year. It is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring Jakarta.
