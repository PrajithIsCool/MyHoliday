Prambanan Temple

Prambanan is the largest Hindu temple compound in Indonesia, located in the Special Region of Yogyakarta. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a magnificent example of 9th-century Central Javanese Hindu architecture. The temple complex is dedicated to the Trimurti, the Hindu trinity of gods: Brahma the Creator, Vishnu the Preserver, and Shiva the Destroyer.

The main temple compound consists of 240 individual temples, with the central shrine dedicated to Shiva rising to a height of 47 meters. The intricate carvings and reliefs on the temple walls depict scenes from Hindu mythology and epics like the Ramayana.

Prambanan is a popular tourist destination, offering visitors a glimpse into the rich cultural and religious heritage of Indonesia. The temple complex is also a significant pilgrimage site for Hindus from all over the world.
